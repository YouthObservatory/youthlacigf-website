YouthLACIGF Fellowship 2018

What is Internet Governance?

It is the development and application by governments, the private sector and civil society, in their respective roles, of shared principles, norms, rules, decision-making processes and programs that shape the evolution and use of the Internet.

What is the YouthLACIGF?


The YouthLACIGF is an initiative born from the growing community of Latin American youth interested in issues related to the Internet. Given the active role that the youth have started taking, this new space is created with multi-sectoral characteristics, as a forum for debate of new ideas and perspectives to encourage the creation of lines of discussion that will be taken to other forums.

Fellowships

On this occasion, a very limited number of partial fellowships will be provided. From June 11 to 22, 2018, interested parties may apply to receive a partial fellowship financed by the sponsors, which consists solely and exclusively of the air ticket (round trip) from the city of residence of the fellow to Buenos Aires, Argentina. Incomplete or late applications will not be accepted.

Privacy Policy

PRIVACY POLICY

In the Youth Observatory we are aware that privacy and intimacy are fundamental rights that need to be promoted, so we want you to know what are the personal data we collect in relation with the development of our activities and how we use it.

What kind of personal data we collect?
We collect personal data through different means, digital and analogic. The personal data we collect is mainly your full name, age, email, organization, stakeholder, photos, videos and in the case of scholarships scan of the identity document (passport).

In the Youth Observatory we have established as communication channel the address odjuventud@gmail.com and the contact form available on our website. The decision to provide information and the type of information you provide through these channels is up to you.

What we do with your personal data?
In the Youth Observatory we collect personal data only with your express and unequivocal consent. We do it to register you in our database to have a channel of communication with you. We will send you newsletters, announcements, events, trainings, etc., so that you can participate in our activities.

In the case of subscriptions made through Internet Society platforms, the data will be processed and stored under the terms and conditions of that organization. We recommend you to enter and see its privacy policy: https://www.internetsociety.org/es/node/14.

What are your rights when you give us your personal data? ‘ARCO’ rights (access, rectification, cancellation and opposition).

  • You have the right to know what are the personal data we have about you and how we use it.
  • There was a change? You have the right to update your personal data.
  • We have wrong information? You have the right to request to us for a rectification of your personal data.
  • Do you want we erase your personal data? You have the right to request to us the elimination of your data.
  • Do not want to being in our database? You have the right to revoke your authorization.

How do you exercise this rights?
To exercise the rights mentioned above you can contact us by mail to: odjuventud@gmail.com, with the subject: “Request ARCO”.

For how long we keep your personal data?
We will only collect, store, use or communicate your personal data for as long as is reasonable and necessary, according to the purpose for which we request your authorization.

How we protect your personal data?
We use reasonable efforts in the treatment of your personal data, taking in consideration the technology we use to prevent the loss, adulteration, consultation, communication, use or not authorized or fraudulent access. However, we can’t guarantee the absolute security of the information.

What happen with your privacy when you visit external websites from the Youth Observatory web?
It is likely that we redirect you from our website to others through links to see content or other websites. However, we are not responsibly of the privacy policies in this websites and we don’t have control on them. We invite you to know what are their policies.

In the Youth Observatory we actively use the social networks and is likely that sometimes you send personal data (name, email, so on.) to us through this channels. We are committed to give an appropriate treatment to this data. However, we are not responsibly of the privacy policies in this websites and we don’t have control on them.

From when this Policy is inforce?
This Privacy Policy is inforced from July (2017) but it could be modificated along the time; if there is significant changes we are committed to inform you before its implementation.
